About Clembert Partners

Bridging law, finance, and cross-border business across Africa.

Our Focus

Clembert Partners is a commercial law firm advising founders, investors, non-bank lenders, and international clients operating across Africa.

The firm focuses on FinTech and non-bank lending, regulatory compliance, company and investment structuring, dispute resolution, and cross-border trade and transactions.

Our work sits at the intersection of law, finance, regulation, and cross-border business. We support clients from market entry through scale, helping them navigate regulatory environments, structure compliant and commercially viable operations, and manage legal and regulatory risk.

We believe that strong legal foundations enable sustainable growth. Our role is to provide clear, practical legal guidance that allows clients to build and operate with confidence.

Mission Statement

To provide clear, practical, and commercially grounded legal guidance that enables founders, lenders, investors, and businesses to operate confidently, comply with regulatory requirements, and build sustainable ventures across Africa.

Vision Statement

To be recognised as a leading African law firm advising on FinTech, lending, regulation, and cross-border business, and to serve as trusted legal counsel to founders, investors, and institutions building the future of finance and commerce across the continent.

Our Team

Ifeanyi Clement Nweke

Managing Partner, Clembert Partners

Ifeanyi Clement Nweke advises founders, investors, non-bank lenders, and international clients on FinTech and non-bank lending, regulatory compliance, commercial and regulatory dispute resolution, corporate structuring, and cross-border transactions across Africa.

His practice sits at the intersection of law, finance, regulation, and cross-border business. He works with clients from market entry through scale, helping them navigate African regulatory environments, structure compliant and commercially viable operations, facilitate cross-border investments, and manage regulatory and commercial disputes.

A core focus of his advisory work is guiding clients in making early structural decisions around ownership, control, governance, and risk — decisions designed to remain sound as businesses grow and regulatory landscapes evolve.

He regularly supports foreign founders and international investors entering African markets, advising on regulatory positioning, investment structuring, compliance frameworks, and transaction strategy across jurisdictions.

Ifeanyi holds an LLB from Nnamdi Azikiwe University and an LLM in International Trade, Business and Investment Law from University of the Western Cape. His academic training in trade and investment law informs his cross-border and regulatory advisory practice across the continent.
